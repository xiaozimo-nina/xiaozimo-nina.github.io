Question Papers


Before you start to do the past paper, I have a few tips:

  • The time limit for paper one is two hours, and for paper two and paper three is two hours and a half.
    You can put an alarm clock next to it to make sure you finish the paper within that time limit.

  • Some of the practice questions will ask you to keep evidence (in screenshots) and some won't,
    so look them in the eye, because evidence will counted as a part of your grade.

  • While you are doing the past papers, please don't look at your phone, books, syllabus, or search on Google, take them as serious as the real exam,
    then check your answers strictly follow the answer key -- so that you can get a more realistic and referential score.

Mark Scheme


Before you start to check your answers, I have a few tips:

  • Before you grade your answers, you can use a different color pen.
    This will help you better distinguish between what you originally wrote and what you added when you saw the answer.

  • You can make a backup of your original files before you modify your computer practice paper, so that if you accidentally break or lose your files, you will have a remedy.

  • If you can't figure out how to solve your computer practice problem by only looking at the mark scheme, you can watch this blogger's video (click here).
    In his video, he will complete the content of the test paper step by step.

Grade Thresholds


Before you start to check your letter grade, I have a few tips:

  • Grades vary slightly from year to year, depending on the difficulty of the paper at the time and how well most people complete it.

  • The assessment objectives (AOs) are:

    1. AO1 Recall, select and communicate knowledge and understanding of ICT
    2. AO2 Apply knowledge, understanding and skills to produce ICT-based solutions
    3. AO3 Analyse, evaluate, make reasoned judgements and present conclusions
